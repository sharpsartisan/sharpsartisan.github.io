Frequently Asked Questions

Is the use of fractal models useful for modelling miRNA regulatory networks?

Using fractal models to model microRNA regulatory networks may be a good idea, but one must be sure that one is modeling a real situation. Self-similarity involves a hierarchical organization of the topology, but it is not certain that the microRNA regulatory network is a hierarchical network or not. It appears to be a network with a high level of degeneracy. In addition, the growing rules are largely unknown, nor is the generative function, nor the scale. Another relevant point is the real pattern of interactions: the competition among microRNAs involve a continuous remodeling of the network and a model that optimises the robustness and degeneracy of the network would be the most accurate for a microRNA network.

Which would be the most appropriate fractal dimension definition to compute the regularity of a binary image?

Fractal dimension can be used to characterise natural landscape features and rock masses, however every geometrical analysis can be comparable. In such a case, the box-counting method is most likely the most stable. Moreover, it is also capable of providing you with a fractal dimension which is related to both the spatial distribution of objects (in the 2D or in the 3D space) and the frequency size distribution of the same objects.

What is the relation between Fractal Space-Time and Scale Relativity?

Fractal space-time has no physical existence. It is only the math model. Do not think motion happens in time, because motion happens in space only and time is its duration.

Are all Wada basin boundaries fractal?

A point X on the basin boundary is a Wada point, if every open neighborhood of X has a nonempty intersection with at least three different basins. The boundary of a basin is called Wada basin boundary. The answer is that a Wada basin is a fractal boundary, but a fractal boundary is not necessarily Wada.

What is the exact difference between self-affine and self-similar fractals?

Self-affinity is distorted self-similarity. In the case of self-similarity, the objects are scaled by the same amount in all directions, but in self-affinity scaling is not necessary identical in all directions. An affine transformation is imposed on a self-affine structure to witness its self-similarity.
